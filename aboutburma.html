About Burma for beginners

Where is Burma?

Many people have asked me where I am from but only a few of them know where my country is when I say I am from Burma (aka Myanmar). Burma is situated in Southeast Asia and is bordered on the north and north-east by China, on the east and south-east by Laos and Thailand, on the south by the Andaman Sea and the Bay of Bengal and on the west by Bangladesh and India.

Google Map of Burma

What has been happening in Burma?

On 1st February, Myanmar’s Military detained President Win Myine, State Counselor Aung San Suu Kyi, and many high-ranking officials. The military’s commander-in-chief Senior General Min Aung Hlaing forcibly replaced the government mandated by the Myanmar People with himself. An eleven-men council now possesses all legislative, administrative, and judiciary power, with no check-and-balance measures for accountability.

We need your attention.

Now, the Myanmar Public is self-mobilizing among themselves in a collective civil disobedience movement to eradicate dictatorship from their land. The military continues to detain prominent activists, kill civilians, burn thousands of innocent civilians’ houses and commit war crimes. We want to tell the world that this coup is not justifiable whatsoever. A year ago (in November 2020), we mandated our leadership for the next five years. And in no way, do we want Min Aung Hlaing and his associates to rule above us.

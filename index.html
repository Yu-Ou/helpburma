My freedom of speech for my people in Burma

Image credit goes to CNN

The girl doing the three-fingered salute in the picture is me.

Why this as the final project?

As a Burmese student who came from a third world country that has suffered the disasters and atrocities as the results of military coup in February 2021, spreading awareness and speaking up is the only way I can do for our people in Myanmar. When I was in Myanmar, I did whatever I could such as protesting and contributing money to organizations/ forces, which are fighting against the military, to restore the democracy, to fight for our freedom and future that has been stolen by the military till I left the country in July 2022. I want to keep fighting for it and I want my existence to be useful even though I am no longer in my home country.

What does the three-fingered salute symbolize?

“A three-fingered salute that originated in the Hunger Games film series has been adopted by activists from Thailand to Myanmar, becoming a symbol of resistance and solidarity for democracy movements across south-east Asia. The gesture, along with popular online memes repurposed as protest signs, are part of a suite of symbols adopted from global popular culture by a new generation of young activists reared on the internet and savvy about making their struggles resonate with audiences abroad.”

The text is from the guardian.com
